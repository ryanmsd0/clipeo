Most clipping guides explain how to edit a clip. That's the problem backwards. Cutting an episode, adding captions, landing a hook: you can learn it in an afternoon. What decides the result is what you do with the clips next. Clipping is a distribution game dressed up as an editing game.

Here's how to start for real, without the sales-pitch version.

## 1. Set a number, not a clip count

"Post 30 clips" isn't a goal, it's a chore. "Hit 5 million views and 2,000 followers in 6 weeks" is one. The number of clips, accounts and campaign days is derived from that figure, never the other way around. If you don't know what result you're aiming for, you'll never know whether the campaign worked.

## 2. Pick the moment, not the edit

A single episode holds five or six stories: the anecdote that surprises, the opinion that divides, the tip people save, the moment of emotion. **80% of the result is decided here**, in the choice of the clip, before a single transition. A flawless edit on a flat moment will do nothing. A strong moment shot decently can blow up. List your angles first, cut second.

## 3. Win the first three seconds

In short-form, the viewer decides in 3 seconds whether they stay. Open with the tension, the promise or the killer line. Never with an intro, never with "hey everyone." If the first three seconds don't plant a question in the viewer's head, the rest of the clip won't get watched.

## 4. The part nobody shows you: distribution

This is where 9 creators out of 10 give up, because it's the opposite of glamorous. A single account posting one clip a day is invisible, full stop. The recommendation engine is saturated by **volume**: multiple accounts, multiple angles, multiple time slots, every day.

And it isn't only a matter of effort. It's logistics: warming up accounts so they don't get throttled, varying the posts, holding the cadence without burning out. It's a real operational craft, and it's exactly what separates "I posted some clips" from "I'm everywhere."

## 5. Accept that your first clips will flop

Nobody says it, so we will: your first ten or twenty clips will do few views. That's normal, it's part of the plan. Launch 3 to 5 angles in parallel, give them a week, then be ruthless. Keep what holds attention (retention rate, shares, followers generated), cut the rest, and pour energy back into what works. Clipping rewards iteration, not the first try.
