The question we get asked most often is the wrong question. It's not "AI generator **or** agency." Both will turn a video into captioned vertical clips, and honestly, in 2026, a tool like Opus does it very well. The cut is a solved problem.

Except the cut was never the work. It's 10% of the work. The other 90% is distribution, and that's where views are won or quietly disappear.

## What an AI generator actually leaves on your plate

You run the tool, you get fifteen clean clips back in ten minutes. Then silence. Nobody tells you what to post, on which account, at what time, how often, or why clip number seven deserves three variants while the rest belong in the trash.

A generator optimizes **one clip**. It does not run **a campaign**. It has no concept of angles tested in parallel, of ramping up volume, of routing viewers back to your long-form content. And its "highlight" is statistical: it spots a spike in audio energy, not the editorial moment that's going to make people know who you are. Those are two different things, and it's usually the second one that does a million views.

The real cost of an AI tool isn't its subscription. It's the hours you, or your community manager, will spend doing the job the tool doesn't.

## What an agency actually sells (and it isn't clips)

Let's be honest, because this industry often isn't: plenty of "clipping agencies" are an Opus subscription and an intern. If that's the deal, keep your money and buy the subscription yourself.

A real agency doesn't sell you edits. It sells you two things AI can't deliver:

1. **A distribution network.** Dozens of warmed-up accounts posting at volume across TikTok, Shorts and Reels to saturate the recommendation engine. A single account posting once a day stays invisible, no matter how good the clip is. The power comes from repetition at scale: that's infrastructure, not software.
2. **A number you can hold us to.** At Clipeo, the view volume is a guaranteed view volume, written into your contract. If we miss it, we refund the difference. A tool commits to nothing: it bills you for exports, not for results.

That mechanism is what generated +160.9M views for Charles & Mélanie across 7 campaigns, and +23.1M for Kyan Khojandi's podcast. Not a better cutting algorithm. Distribution, run on purpose.

## The only honest test to settle it

Ask yourself three questions, without lying to yourself:

- Do you have the **time** to post and analyze every day, every week?
- Do you already have the **accounts** and the consistency to exist at volume?
- Are you ready to iterate for weeks before it takes off?

Three times yes: get an AI generator, it's the right tool for you, and paying an agency would be a waste. A single no anywhere: what you're missing isn't an editing tool, it's the machine behind it.
